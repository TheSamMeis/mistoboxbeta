Altitude & Variables

"The altitude at which coffee is grown and the microclimate are complex variables that contribute to how a coffee will taste. "

The altitude at which a coffee is grown plays an essential role in developing sugars, acids, and complex flavors that will ultimately lead to a delicious cup of coffee. It is believed that higher altitudes can slow the development process of cherries, causing more distinctly sweet and balanced cup. This slower development also results in more dense seeds, which will roast more evenly and produce tastier coffee. When determining what coffee to buy, you will often see the altitude listed on the bag. Look for coffees that range from 1000-2000 meters.

The specific weather, such as rainfall patterns and climate of a region also play a role in the growth and development of the coffee cherries.
