Coffee Varieties

"Coffee has a unique benefit of being as simple or complex as you make it. There are a few concepts that will help you understand brewing theory more thoroughly."

Coffee can be classified in various groups. Most commonly, we know that coffee can be distinguished as Arabica or Robusta. These are referring to the species of coffee. Specialty Coffee is made up of coffees that are from the Arabica species. We can classify coffees even further than just by species. These are what are referred to as varieties.

Just like apples can be classified by their variety, so can coffee. Think about how Granny Smith, Honeycrisp and Pink Lady are all varieties of apples. Each one has certain characteristics that we associate with it. Granny Smith are green and round, medium in size, tart, juicy and great for baking with. Honeycrisp are sweet, larger in size, and have a different texture.

Think about coffee the same way. Each variety has different genetic traits that can determine how a coffee will taste, how much each coffee each shrub can produce every year, how resistant the plant is to disease, and even how tall the shrub can grow.
